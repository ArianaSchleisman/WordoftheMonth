September Word of the Month: Navigate (v) to plan and direct the course of a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle; to find one's way.

On many of the pursuits I've been part of lately, there have been limited "markers." Clients just don't know what to ask for, but they know they need something. Uncertainty can often carry an air of anxiety, and our ability to navigate through that anxiety is a critical first step in turning ambiguous requests into meaningful outcomes. The truth is, our clients may also be uncertain and anxious, and demonstrating cool confidence will solidify their trust in us.

Once we've collected ourselves and accepted that the ask is going to be ambiguous, what next? Well, the great news about ambiguity isâ€¦ it's ambiguous! Rather than seeing the lack of direction as a roadblock or a frustration, reframe it as an opportunity to get creative. Make some (reasonable) assumptions and give pie-in-the-sky ideas; get the conversation started so the client has something to react to. Even if you miss the unmarked mark, at least you've started to narrow in on an outcome.

Navigating isn't about having a perfect map; it's about being willing to examine your surroundings, make informed choices, and adjust your course as you go. Be bold, be confident, be humble, and respect your new environment.

As we move into what I know will be a complex and challenging season, I have complete faith in our collective ability to navigate whatever comes our way. Your creativity, resilience, and willingness to lean into uncertainty are what make this team extraordinary.

3. Cross out everything you cannot reasonably influence or control
